In defense of the inclusion of Joshua 21:36 and 37 in editions not
strictly based on manuscripts that lack them, i.e. in editions not
strictly based on LC or AC:

1. Though LC & AC lack these verse, there are significant witnesses for these verses.

2. When we look at the text including these verses, a scribal error omitting them
seems quite plausible due to the fact that both verses 35 and 37 end with
the phrase
וְאֶת־מִגְרָשֶׁ֑הָ עָרִ֖ים אַרְבַּֽע׃.

3. These verses are related to text found in 1 Chronicles 6:63 and 64.
In particular, words 1–10 of the 12 words of the insertion
(וּמִמַּטֵּ֣ה ... וְאֶת־מִגְרָשֶׁ֑הָ)
nearly match words 6–8 and 10–16 of the 16
words of 1C 6:63 and 64.
(Here “words” means chanted words, i.e. a maqaf
compound counts as a single word.)
