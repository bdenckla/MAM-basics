## Masora-specific qualifiers

| Suffix | Meaning |

| -מסורת (e.g. מסורת-א) | the Masora annotations in that manuscript |
| -כתיב (e.g. ל-כתיב) | the ketib (written consonantal text) in that manuscript |
| -קרי (e.g. ל-קרי) | the qere (read form) in that manuscript |

| ל-גדולות | large-letter list in L |

## Where these definitions come from

1. **MAM-for-Acc Accordance header**: `../MAM-for-Acc/in/01.Aleppo Codex Apparatus.header.html` — "Abbreviations and Bibliographic Sources" and "Manuscripts" sections. Covers A, B, C, L, L1, L34, S, S1, Y and all editions/scholars.
2. **MAM-for-Acc sigil mapping code**: `../MAM-for-Acc/acc_endoc_find_translation_errors.py` — `_AUTHORITY_DIC1` dict (lines ~458–568) maps Hebrew sigla to English `{{tag}}` equivalents.
3. **Wikisource נספחים (appendices)**: `https://he.wikisource.org/wiki/ויקיטקסט:מבוא_למקרא_על_פי_המסורה/נספחים` — defines newer sigla (ל-א, etc.) not present in the Accordance edition.

Note: The Accordance edition predates some sigla now used on Wikisource (ל-א, ק13, גסטר1). These newer sigla appear in expanded נוסח notes but are absent from both the Accordance header and the translation mapping code.
